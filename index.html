About Project

The capital of independent Azerbaijan, a rapidly growing energy and transportation hub and a venue for impressive international events in culture and sports, Baku still remains one of the world’s best-kept secrets. Fuad Akhundov’s non-traditional tours and lectures is another attempt to unveil the place where Europe meets Asia in a very peculiar and harmonious manner.

Heritage buildings and stories of their original owners offer an insight into Baku’s storied past, not in mere facts and figures, but, rather, through human fates. Within this project, Fuad is happy to globally offer so-called “Teleporting to Baku” lectures offering the same stories flavored with anecdotes and poetry.

About Fuad

Fuad Akhundov is an enthusiastic researcher of Baku’s history and architecture and a self-trained tour guide. Born in Baku in 1968, Fuad is a graduate of the Oriental Department at Baku State University. He got his Master in Public Administration from the Kennedy School of Government, Harvard University, and the Master of Arts in Education from the University of Toronto.

That being said, Fuad’s passion for Baku’s storied past trumps over all his background and occupation. What started as a hobby, gradually evolved into articles, lectures, films and, above that all, his untraditional tours in old parts of Baku where each and every spot is presented in a ‘now and then’ juxtaposition thanks to tons of historic photos Fuad is carrying in his huge portfolio.

Even though Fuad Akhundov lives in Canada, he is always ready to present a lively interactive lecture on Baku’s history in any part of the world where there is an interest to this truly fascinating city. One thing he knows for sure: Bakuvians are Bakuvians for the rest of their lives, no matter where they happen to live now. So wherever they are, Fuad is ready to convey the message of your native city, Baku.
